# Psychology insights & reminders

*Use this as a dumping ground for insights about psychology & personal development that you'd like to remember and/or be reminded of periodically.*

## Emotional Processing & The Body

- Numbing doesn't eliminate pain, it reschedules it. The nervous system always collects its due.
- Feeling "fine" can mask deep emotional wear. Numbness is often quiet burnout; your nervous system signals depletion before collapse.
- Avoided emotions relocate silently into behavior and body. What you don't feel today quietly decides how you think, act, and cope tomorrow.
- The way out is through. Emotions are meant to move (e-motion = energy in motion). They only get stuck when we resist them.
- Your body keeps the score and tells the truth. Anxiety is often unprocessed activation. Depression is often unexpressed anger or grief. Listen to symptoms as messages, not enemies.
- Between stimulus and response there is a space. In that space is your power. Reaction is automatic; response is chosen. Healing involves learning to widen that gap.

## Specific Emotions

- Anger is information about boundaries. It's not dangerous—suppressing it is. Healthy anger says 'this matters' and 'I matter.'
- Grief is not a problem to solve. It's love with nowhere to go. The depth of grief reflects the depth of love.
- Fear often points toward what matters most. The things you're most afraid to do are often the things you most need to do. Fear isn't always a stop sign, sometimes it's a sign you're heading toward growth.
- Resentment is anger at yourself for saying yes when you meant no. It's the cost of abandoning your boundaries to keep the peace.
- Guilt says 'I did something bad.' Shame says 'I am bad.' Guilt can motivate repair; shame only motivates hiding.
- Beauty is meaning.

## Boundaries, Relationships & Authenticity

- Being needed often costs your rest and presence. Constantly responding to others rewires your nervous system. Health and wholeness requires protecting your focus, energy, and presence.
- You're confining yourself inside an electric fence that stopped working years ago—restraining yourself from your truth for no real reason. Your breakthrough isn't on the other side of productivity or success or self-improvement. It's on the other side of that text you're not sending. That call you're not making. That "I miss you" stuck in your throat.
- Vulnerability is not weakness—it's the birthplace of connection, creativity, and change. What we don't own, we can't transform.
- People-pleasing is self-abandonment disguised as kindness. You can be kind and still be yourself. The right people will stay; the wrong ones leaving is the gift.
- Choose character for the people you gather around you. And you can easily evaluate character by criticizing them and seeing how they respond / receive it.

## Self-Perception & Inner Work

- The parts of yourself you judge most harshly in others are often the exiled parts of yourself. What you condemn externally often lives unowned internally.
- Self-esteem says 'I'm good/better than.' Self-compassion says 'I'm human, and that's enough.' One requires comparison; the other requires nothing.
- Perfectionism isn't about high standards—it's about protection. It says 'If I'm perfect, I won't be criticized, rejected, or abandoned.' But perfectionism makes connection impossible because it won't let you be seen.
- Comparison obscures your own path. You're comparing your internal experience to everyone else's external performance—measuring your rough draft against their highlight reels.

## Energy, Rest & Action

- If your mind is loud: Write.
  If your mind is empty: Read.
  If your mind is racing: Walk.
  If your mind is tired: Sleep.
  If your mind is sharp: Build.
  Most problems are just mismatched energy.
  Get the inputs right, the rest tends to follow.

- Every day, you need 3 wins:
  - A physical win (walking, running, workout, swimming)
  - A mental win (reading, writing, creating, learning)
  - A spiritual win (praying, meditating, studying, growing, processing)

- Rest is not earned. It's required. Your worth is not measured by your productivity. You are not a machine that needs to justify its existence through output.

- Motivation doesn't create action—action creates motivation. Waiting to feel ready is a trap. Start messy. Momentum builds as you move.
